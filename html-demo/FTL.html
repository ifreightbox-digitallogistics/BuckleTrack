    Lets understand Full Truck Load​
  • FTL stands for Full Truck Load, indicating the utilization of an entire truck's capacity for a single shipment.
  • It is chosen when the cargo volume exceeds what can be accommodated by LTL (Less-Than-Truckload) shipping, where multiple smaller shipments are consolidated.
  • FTL is preferred for expedited delivery needs or when the cargo requires dedicated handling due to its size or sensitivity.
  • Advantages include faster transit times, reduced handling, and lower risk of damage or loss compared to LTL shipments.
  • FTL can be more expensive as the cost is borne solely by the customer, unlike LTL where costs are shared among multiple shipments.
